Content Knowledge

  1. Explain what a residual plot of the model residuals versus predicted values might look like when a model that violates the linearity assumption.

The residual plot would have a systematic relationship, generally having a U shape, inverse U shape, or linear.

  1. Explain what a residual plot of studentized deleted residuals versus predicted values might look like when a model that violates the homoscedasticity (equal variance) assumption.

The spread of residuals would not be equal around zero (e.g., the residuals might display a cone shape).

  1. True/False: Regression models may be biased even when all model assumptions are met. Explain your answer

True. Outlier cases that are the result of data entry or other error in the system (i.e., research design) may have undue influence on the regression results.

  1. True/False: Outlier cases distort regression models and should be removed prior to interpretation. Explain your answer.

False. Outlier cases should be investigated for their influence on the regression model. Further, true outlier cases can be quite meaningful. Discarding data should be done with care, not haphazardly. If a case is found to have undue influence on the regression results, and the case is either substantively not meaningful or can be traced back to an error, removal is likely warranted.

Interpret the model below. Write as if you were reporting for publication.

When mothers had a weight of zero (an impossible value), and the youngest child was zero years old, their expected weight was -89.23 pounds, with a 95% confidence interval spanning -140.24 pounds to -38.22 pounds. Thus, while the coefficeint was significant, it was estimated with considerable uncertainty. The weight of the youngest child was expected to increase approximately 0.43 pounds for every one pound heavier the mother was, with a 95% CI ranging from 0.19 to 0.67, controlling for the age of the child. Given that the CI did not cross zero, this value was significant (\(t(21) = 3.69, p = 0.001\)). Age was entered primarily as a control variable and thus was not interpreted.

Overall, the model including age and mothers weight accounted for approximately 72% of the total variability in the weight of youngest children, which was also significant \(F(2, 21) = 27.39, p < .001)\).
